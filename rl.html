Reinforcement Learning with Action Trajectory Proposals


Paper

I trained an agent to drive a car around a racetrack as fast as possible using deep Q learning in OpenAI Gym's CarRacing-v0 environment. To speed up training, the agent generates action trajectory proposals that have a higher chance of being the correct actions to complete subtasks in the environment. Instead of randomizing actions during exploration steps, exploration actions are autocorrelated. To find out more, please see the paper linked above.

Other tricks done to make training more efficient include decaying epsilon in a cosine wave, and replacing grayscale conversion with simply extracting the green layer (this is environment specific). I had a hunch that for complex state spaces, decaying epsilon in a cosine wave might speed up training as it can quickly get the agent into harder to reach regions of the state space. This has produced better results than epsilon decay.
