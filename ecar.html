Electric Racecar Drivetrain

I designed and manufactured an in-hub drivetrain for a Formula SAE Electric racecar. This is a unit that serves as the motor, gearbox, and brake, and it fits inside each of the front wheels. The motor, mounted to the back of the upright, drives a planetary gear reduction with the ring gear as the final output to the wheel. Components were put through extensive finite element analysis to make sure they don't fail under driving loads while being as lightweight as possible.

This was Wisconsin Racing's first year going electric, and everyone told us we were biting off more than we can chew by building an AWD car with four independant wheel motors. However, we stuck with it and ended up winning first place in the design category, along with several other awards at FSAE Lincoln 2017!

Tech Specs:
  • 15 kW, 180 Nm of torque per wheel
  • 6:1 gear reduction
  • 17 lbs. per wheel
  • Inverted brake calipers
